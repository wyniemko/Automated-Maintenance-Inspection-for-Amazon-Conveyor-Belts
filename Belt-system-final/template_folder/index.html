Automated Maintenance Inspection for Amazon Conveyor Belts




Belt Condition Evaluation Critera
Edge The number of edges detected on the belt surface. A lower number of edges is indicative of a smoother surface, while a higher number of edges may indicate damage or debris on the belt.
Vertices The number of vertices detected on the belt surface. A higher number of vertices is indicative of a rougher surface, which can cause wear and tear on the belt over time.
Convexity A measure of how 'bulging' or 'hollow' the belt surface is. A convex surface is one that curves outward, while a concave surface curves inward. In general, a more convex surface is less likely to cause damage to the belt.
Solidity The ratio of the area of the belt surface to the area of its convex hull. This measures how 'solid' the belt surface is, with higher values indicating a smoother, less irregular surface.
Benchmark Data Thresholds
Edge Threshold 50
Vertices Threshold 200
Convexity Threshold 0.5
Solidity Threshold 0.8
